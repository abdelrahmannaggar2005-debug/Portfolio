The Challenge

Airlines can run full flights and still lose money on a route once fuel, crew, maintenance, insurance, and overhead are factored in. The final solution provides a comprehensive Business Intelligence platform that enables stakeholders to monitor route profitability, analyze operational efficiency, evaluate passenger and booking behavior, and identify exactly which cost drivers erode margin — through interactive, data-driven reporting.

My Approach

  1. Data Foundation: Extracted and cleaned raw flight-operations data from a 9-table relational database using Python and Pandas, then established a scalable analytical model in Power BI.
  2. Analytics Development: Created advanced KPIs, cost-category breakdowns, load factor metrics, and profitability calculations using DAX best practices.
  3. Decision Intelligence: Built a route-level profitability waterfall and drill-through decomposition tree enabling stakeholders to trace revenue down to the exact cost line responsible for margin loss on any route.

The Impact

The resulting platform evolved beyond a traditional dashboard into a complete route-profitability intelligence system. Stakeholders can identify which routes are quietly bleeding cash, pinpoint the exact cost driver behind it, and compare performance across seasons, demand levels, and aircraft types before making strategic network decisions.

What I Learned

  • ▸
    Business value comes from enabling decisions, not simply displaying metrics.
  • ▸
    Clean, well-modeled data is the foundation of any scalable analytics solution — the ETL layer is where most of the real work happens.
  • ▸
    A well-built cost waterfall can turn an abstract profit number into a clear, actionable story.
