The Challenge

An online retail company was experiencing 35% annual customer churn with no clear understanding of why customers were leaving or which segments were most at risk. Marketing was spending $500K annually on blanket retention campaigns with minimal ROI.

My Approach

I took full ownership of this ambiguous problem. First, I conducted stakeholder interviews to understand business priorities. Then, I designed a three-phase analytics framework:

  1. Discovery: Pulled 2 years of transactional data covering 15K customers and 150K+ orders. Performed exploratory data analysis to identify patterns in purchasing behavior, average order value, and purchase frequency.
  2. Segmentation: Applied RFM methodology to create 5 distinct customer personas ranging from "Champions" (high value, recent purchases) to "At-Risk" (high value, declining activity). This gave marketing clear targets.
  3. Prediction: Built survival models to forecast churn probability at 30, 60, and 90-day intervals. This allowed proactive intervention before customers churned.

The Impact

The dashboard I built became the central tool for weekly retention meetings. Marketing shifted from spray-and-pray to targeted campaigns per segment. Within 6 months, churn dropped from 35% to 28% (20% reduction), saving an estimated $2.4M in lifetime customer value. The VP of Marketing said it was the "most actionable analysis" they'd received.
